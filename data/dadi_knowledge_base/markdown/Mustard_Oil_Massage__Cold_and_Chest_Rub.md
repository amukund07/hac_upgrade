## 📖 Summary

Mustard oil heated with garlic and applied to chest, back, and feet is one of the most common cold remedies across North India. Its erucic acid and allyl isothiocyanate have warming, antibacterial, and decongestant properties. Applied to the feet with garlic at night, it was the original 'vapour rub'.

## 🌿 Ingredients

- Mustard Oil
- Garlic
- Ajwain (Carom Seeds)
- Camphor

## 📋 How to Use

1. Heat 2 tbsp mustard oil with 3-4 crushed garlic cloves until garlic turns golden
2. Add 1/2 tsp ajwain seeds, heat 30 more seconds
3. Cool to warm (test on wrist), apply to chest and back while warm
4. For congestion: add 1 small camphor piece to oil, apply to chest only
5. For feet: rub warm garlic-mustard oil on soles and toes before bed, wear socks
6. For babies: use warm (not hot) mustard oil massage daily in winter for warmth

## 💚 Health Benefits

Cold, Congestion, Cough, Chest Tightness, Muscle Aches, Warming

## 🧬 Scientific Backing

- Allyl isothiocyanate: Creates heat through TRPV1 receptor activation (similar to capsaicin)
- Selenium in mustard oil: Antioxidant and antifungal
- Garlic allicin: Antimicrobial against respiratory pathogens
- Counter-irritant effect: Increases local blood flow and reduces deep congestion

## ⚠️ Contraindications & Safety

- ⚠️ Do not apply near eyes or on broken skin
- ⚠️ Allergy to mustard (rare)
