## 📖 Summary

Oil pulling — swishing sesame or coconut oil in the mouth for 10-20 minutes — is described in the Charaka Samhita as Gandusha. It is one of the most extensively scientifically validated Ayurvedic practices, with peer-reviewed evidence for dental health benefits comparable to chlorhexidine mouthwash.

## 🌿 Ingredients

- Sesame/Coconut Oil: 1 tablespoon
- Sesame Oil (traditional)
- Coconut Oil (alternative)

## 📋 How to Use

1. On waking (before eating, drinking, or brushing), take 1 tbsp sesame or coconut oil
2. Swish vigorously in mouth for 15-20 minutes — pulling between teeth
3. The oil will turn white and thin as it emulsifies saliva and pulls bacteria
4. Spit into trash bin — NOT the sink (can clog pipes)
5. Rinse mouth with warm salt water
6. Brush teeth as normal afterward
7. Do this daily for 30 days and notice the difference in oral freshness, gum health, teeth whitening

- Comparable to 0.2% chlorhexidine mouthwash in reducing Streptococcus mutans count
- Saponification: Oil emulsifies bacterial cell membranes — physical removal mechanism
- Reduces plaque index and gingival scores comparable to chlorhexidine (RCT)
- Lauric acid (coconut): Potent antimicrobial against oral pathogens
