## 📖 Summary

Unani medicine (Tibb-e-Unani, or 'Greek medicine') was transmitted from Greece through Persia and the Arab world, adopted and dramatically expanded by Indian Hakims over 800 years. The Indian Unani tradition produced unique formulations blending Greco-Arabic concepts with Indian medicinal plants, creating a distinct healing system practiced by 45,000+ practitioners in India today.

## 🌺 Origin Story

Ibn Sina's Canon of Medicine (1025 CE) is the foundational Unani text. When Mughal emperors brought Persian physicians to Delhi, they encountered Indian medicinal plants, developed new combinations, and created a unique Indo-Unani tradition. Hakim Ajmal Khan (1868-1927) documented thousands of Unani formulas and founded the Unani Medical College in Delhi.
