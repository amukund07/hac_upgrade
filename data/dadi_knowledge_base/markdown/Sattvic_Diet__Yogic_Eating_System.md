## 📖 Summary

Ayurveda classifies food into three categories based on their effect on mind and body: Sattvic (pure, light, promotes clarity), Rajasic (stimulating, activating), and Tamasic (heavy, dulling, promoting inertia). This 5000-year-old food classification system remarkably aligns with modern nutritional science on gut-brain connection and neuroinflammation.

## 📋 How to Use

1. Sattvic foods: fresh fruits, vegetables, dairy, whole grains, legumes, nuts, honey
2. Eat freshly cooked food within 4 hours of cooking (prana is highest)
3. Avoid reheated, stale, or processed food (tamasic)
4. Eat in calm environment, with gratitude, without screens or arguments (emotional sattvic)
5. Avoid overeating — fill stomach: 50% food, 25% water, 25% empty (air space)
6. Rajasic to limit: excess spice, caffeine, onion, garlic (stimulating — fine in moderation)
7. Tamasic to avoid: meat, alcohol, heavily processed foods, stale food

## 🧬 Scientific Backing

- Gut-brain axis: Diet directly modulates neurotransmitter production (90% of serotonin in gut)

- Mediterranean/plant-rich diet (similar to sattvic) reduces depression risk by 33% (meta-analysis)
- Mindful eating reduces cortisol and improves digestive enzyme production
