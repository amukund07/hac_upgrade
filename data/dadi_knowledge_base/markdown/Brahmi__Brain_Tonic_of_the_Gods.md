## 📖 Summary

Brahmi is Ayurveda's foremost brain herb, used for thousands of years to enhance memory, learning, and concentration. Children in gurukuls were given Brahmi before lessons. Modern neuroscience confirms it grows dendrites, reduces anxiety, and protects against Alzheimer's through multiple mechanisms.

## 🌺 Origin Story

Brahmi is named after Brahma, the god of knowledge and creation. Ancient texts say sages consumed Brahmi to memorise the Vedas — texts running to hundreds of thousands of verses committed entirely to memory.

## 🌿 Ingredients

## 📋 How to Use

1. Brahmi milk: 1/4 tsp brahmi powder in warm milk with honey, take before bed
2. Brahmi ghee (Brahmi Ghrita): traditional classical preparation — brahmi cooked in ghee
3. Brahmi hair oil: boil brahmi leaves in coconut oil, cool, massage scalp weekly
4. For children's memory: 1/4 tsp brahmi + 1/4 tsp ashwagandha in warm milk daily
5. Brahmi tea: boil 5-6 fresh leaves, drink with honey for clarity before study/work

## 💚 Health Benefits

Memory, Focus, Anxiety, ADHD, Epilepsy, Hair, Alzheimer's Prevention

## 🔬 Active Compounds

- Bacosides A & B
- Bacopasaponins
- Alkaloids (brahmine, herpestine)
- Flavonoids

## 🧬 Scientific Backing

- Meta-analysis (9 RCTs): Significant improvement in free recall memory vs placebo
- Reduces cortisol equivalent to 300mg ashwagandha in direct comparison
- Inhibits acetylcholinesterase — same mechanism as Alzheimer's drugs (donepezil)
- Promotes dendrite branching and synaptogenesis in hippocampus
- ADHD: Significant reduction in ADHD scores in children (double-blind RCT)

## ⚠️ Contraindications & Safety

- ⚠️ May slow processing speed initially — give 4-6 weeks for full effect
- ⚠️ Caution with thyroid medication
