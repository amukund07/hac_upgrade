## 📖 Summary

Ritucharya is the Ayurvedic seasonal routine — a complete framework for adapting diet, lifestyle, exercise, and herbal support across India's six classical seasons (Ritu). Just as Dinacharya syncs the body with the day, Ritucharya syncs the body with the year. Failure to adapt to seasonal transitions is considered a primary cause of disease in Ayurveda.

## 🌺 Origin Story

The Ashtanga Hridayam dedicates an entire chapter to Ritucharya, describing in detail how each organ system is stressed differently in each season, and how food, herbs, and routines should shift to maintain balance.

## 📋 How to Use

1. Shishira (Jan-Feb, late winter): Warming foods — til, ghee, wheat, new jaggery. Vigorous exercise. Hot water bathing.
2. Vasanta (Mar-Apr, spring): Detox season — light foods, bitter greens, Triphala, avoid oily heavy foods. Vaman (emesis therapy) classically recommended.
3. Grishma (May-Jun, summer): Cooling foods — coconut water, buttermilk, cucumber, rice, avoid chilli. Reduce exercise intensity. Cold water bathing.
4. Varsha (Jul-Aug, monsoon): Digestive fire lowest — light soups, moong dal, ginger. Avoid raw vegetables and leafy greens. Consume garlic, rock salt.
5. Sharad (Sep-Oct, autumn): Purification season — Virechan (purgation). Light, dry, bitter, astringent foods. Moon-charged water (leave water in silver vessel overnight).
6. Hemanta (Nov-Dec, early winter): Nourishing season — dairy, sesame, warm oils. Abhyanga daily. Rasayana herbs — ashwagandha, chyawanprash.
