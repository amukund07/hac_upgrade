## 🌺 Origin Story

The word 'zero' derives from Sanskrit 'Shunya' (the void) via Arabic 'sifr'. When Arab traders carried Indian numerals back to Baghdad in the 9th century, the concept of zero transformed Islamic mathematics — and through them, European mathematics. Without this transmission, the Renaissance may never have happened.
