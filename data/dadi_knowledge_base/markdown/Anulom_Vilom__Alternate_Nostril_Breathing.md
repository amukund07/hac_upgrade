## 📖 Summary

Alternate nostril breathing (Nadi Shodhana) balances the left (Ida — lunar) and right (Pingala — solar) nadis, corresponding to the parasympathetic and sympathetic nervous systems. It is the most studied yoga breathing technique with robust evidence for blood pressure reduction, anxiety relief, and cognitive enhancement.

## 📋 How to Use

1. Sit comfortably, form Vishnu mudra: fold index and middle fingers, use thumb for right nostril, ring finger for left
2. Exhale completely
3. Close right nostril with thumb, inhale through left for 4 counts
4. Close both nostrils, retain breath for 4-8 counts (optional for beginners: skip retention)
5. Release thumb, close left nostril with ring finger, exhale through right for 8 counts
6. Inhale through right for 4 counts
7. Close both, retain 4-8 counts
8. Exhale through left for 8 counts = 1 complete cycle
9. Do 10-20 cycles. Build time over weeks. Ratio: inhale 1 : retain 2-4 : exhale 2

Blood Pressure, Stress, Anxiety, Sleep, Asthma, Respiratory, Heart

## 🧬 Scientific Backing

- Significantly reduces systolic and diastolic blood pressure in hypertensive patients (RCT)
- EEG shows synchronised alpha wave activity across both hemispheres post-practice
- Right nostril breathing activates left brain (logic); left nostril activates right brain (creativity)
- Improves spatial memory and reaction time vs control group
- Reduces anxiety comparable to lorazepam in dental patients (RCT)

## ⚠️ Contraindications & Safety

- ⚠️ Avoid breath retention if hypertensive
- ⚠️ Avoid with severe nasal congestion (use decongestant first)
